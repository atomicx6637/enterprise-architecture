# Enterprise Architecture 2026 OKRs

## Overview
These OKRs represent Year 1 of our 5-year vision journey, focused on building the foundation and credibility needed to become the utility's trusted strategic partner. The objectives balance building internal capability (our team and tools), establishing demand management visibility, and demonstrating measurable value through business partnerships.

---

## Objective 1: Establish Enterprise Demand Management Visibility

**Why This Matters:** To become proactive strategic partners, we must first see what's happening across the enterprise. This objective creates the foundation for capacity planning, portfolio optimization, and strategic alignment.

### Key Results:

**KR 1.1: Achieve 80% visibility into enterprise technology investments**
- **Measure:** Inventory and track 80% of planned IT/OT initiatives >$100K in EA demand management system
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Complete portfolio discovery across business units by Q2
  - Establish intake integration with PMO, IT portfolio, and capital planning by Q3
  - Maintain updated portfolio view with monthly refresh cadence by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Lead, Demand Management
- **Dependencies:** Collaboration with PMO, IT Planning, Business Planning

**KR 1.2: Create executive portfolio dashboard with strategic alignment insights**
- **Measure:** Launch and socialize dashboard showing portfolio health, capacity, strategic alignment, and risk
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Define dashboard requirements with CIO and business VPs by Q1
  - Deploy initial dashboard with at least 50 initiatives tracked by Q2
  - Present portfolio insights at monthly IT leadership meetings starting Q3
  - Achieve 75% executive user adoption (regular dashboard access) by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Lead, Portfolio Analytics
- **Dependencies:** Data integration from project systems, alignment with IT leadership on metrics

**KR 1.3: Establish demand intake process with 60% adoption**
- **Measure:** 60% of new initiatives >$250K flow through EA demand intake process
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Document and publish streamlined intake process by Q1
  - Pilot intake with 2-3 friendly business units in Q2
  - Integrate intake into governance/stage gate process by Q3
  - Track intake vs. total new initiatives monthly, achieve 60% by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Program Manager
- **Dependencies:** Governance alignment, business unit champion engagement, light tooling

## Objective 2: Build Business Partnership Capabilities and Credibility

**Why This Matters:** To be valued strategic partners, we must speak the language of business, engage proactively, and demonstrate measurable value. This objective develops our team's business acumen and proves our impact through tangible outcomes.

### Key Results:

**KR 2.1: Partner on 12+ business cases with documented value contribution**
- **Measure:** EA team actively contributes to at least 12 business cases, with documented value (e.g., cost avoidance, risk reduction, alignment to strategy)
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Identify partnership opportunities through demand intake and business unit relationships
  - For each business case, document EA's specific contribution (feasibility analysis, cost optimization, strategic alignment, integration strategy)
  - Collect stakeholder feedback (survey or interview) confirming EA value
  - Create case study library showcasing business partnership examples by Q4
- **Owner:** All Enterprise Architects (distributed target: 3 per architect)
- **Dependencies:** Business unit willingness to engage, discipline to document contributions

**KR 2.2: Increase business stakeholder satisfaction from baseline to 4.0/5.0**
- **Measure:** Conduct stakeholder satisfaction survey in Q1 (baseline) and Q4 (progress check)
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Survey business and IT leaders who have engaged with EA (target 20+ responses)
  - Measure: responsiveness, business understanding, value delivered, partnership effectiveness
  - Achieve average satisfaction score of 4.0/5.0 or higher by Q4
  - Identify and address top 2-3 improvement areas from feedback
- **Owner:** EA Director
- **Dependencies:** Sufficient engagement volume to survey, honest feedback from stakeholders

**KR 2.3: Develop business acumen across EA team**
- **Measure:** 100% of EA team members complete business acumen development and demonstrate proficiency
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Each architect completes 40+ hours of business-focused learning (utility operations, regulatory environment, financial planning, customer experience, grid operations)
  - Each architect shadows/embeds with a business unit for 2+ weeks in Q2-Q3
  - Each architect presents a "business unit deep dive" to EA team by Q4, demonstrating understanding of unit's strategy, challenges, and priorities
  - Team self-assessment shows improved confidence in business conversations (pre/post survey)
- **Owner:** EA Director (program design), Individual Architects (execution)
- **Dependencies:** Business unit cooperation for shadowing, budget for training

**KR 2.4: Establish EA presence in strategic planning cycles**
- **Measure:** EA participates in strategic planning for at least 4 major business functions
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Identify annual strategic planning cycles for key business units (Grid Ops, Customer Ops, IT, Engineering, etc.) by Q1
  - Secure invitations to participate in at least 4 strategic planning sessions in Q2-Q3
  - Contribute capability assessments, technology roadmap inputs, or constraint/opportunity analysis
  - Document follow-up actions from each planning session (e.g., architecture assessments, feasibility studies)
- **Owner:** EA Director + Senior Architects
- **Dependencies:** Relationships with business leaders, credibility to earn invitations

## Objective 3: Accelerate Delivery Through Architecture Enablement

**Why This Matters:** We must prove that architecture adds velocity, not friction. By providing tools, frameworks, and patterns that help teams move faster with confidence, we demonstrate tangible operational excellence value.

### Key Results:

**KR 3.1: Deploy architecture enablement toolset and achieve 70% team adoption**
- **Measure:** Launch suite of EA tools (reference architectures, design patterns, decision frameworks, standards library) and achieve 70% utilization by project teams
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Inventory and prioritize enablement needs through project team interviews by Q1
  - Publish initial toolset (at least 5 reusable assets) on accessible platform by Q2
  - Conduct 4+ enablement workshops/lunch-n-learns in Q3-Q4
  - Track tool downloads/usage and survey adoption; achieve 70% awareness and 50% active use by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Enablement Lead
- **Dependencies:** Platform for sharing assets (SharePoint, Confluence, etc.), time to create quality assets

**KR 3.2: Reduce architecture review cycle time by 30%**
- **Measure:** Decrease average time from architecture review request to decision/approval from baseline to 30% reduction
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Establish baseline review cycle time in Q1 (track 20+ reviews)
  - Implement streamlined review process with clear decision criteria and SLAs by Q2
  - Use enablement tools to help teams "self-service" low-risk decisions
  - Measure cycle time monthly; achieve 30% reduction by Q4
  - Maintain or improve quality (track rework or issues attributed to architecture decisions)
- **Owner:** EA Program Manager
- **Dependencies:** Governance process alignment, team discipline on SLAs

**KR 3.3: Document avoided costs and accelerated delivery from EA engagement**
- **Measure:** Track and report at least $2M in documented avoided costs or measurable delivery acceleration
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Establish methodology for quantifying EA value (cost avoidance, schedule acceleration, risk reduction) by Q1
  - For each significant EA engagement, capture value metrics (e.g., identified vendor consolidation saving $500K, integration strategy saved 3 months)
  - Create quarterly value report for IT and business leadership
  - Achieve at least $2M cumulative documented value by year-end
- **Owner:** EA Director + all architects (capture), EA Analyst (reporting)
- **Dependencies:** Discipline to document value in real-time, conservative estimation methodology

## Objective 4: Develop a High-Performing, Adaptable EA Team

**Why This Matters:** Our vision requires a team culture that is collaborative, agile, and continuously learning. We must invest in our people and create an environment where architects thrive as both technical experts and business partners.

### Key Results:

**KR 4.1: Achieve 90% EA team engagement score**
- **Measure:** Conduct quarterly team engagement/satisfaction pulse surveys and achieve average score of 90% favorable responses
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Survey covers: clarity of direction, collaboration, tools/resources, growth opportunities, leadership support
  - Address top issues raised within 30 days of each survey
  - Track engagement trend across all 4 quarters
  - Maintain 90%+ favorable by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Director
- **Dependencies:** Psychological safety for honest feedback, resources to address issues

**KR 4.2: Establish EA community of practice with cross-enterprise participation**
- **Measure:** Launch EA community and achieve participation from at least 30 cross-functional members (project managers, developers, business analysts, IT leaders)
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Define community purpose, format, and engagement model by Q1
  - Launch with kickoff event and monthly cadence (e.g., EA office hours, architecture talks, case study reviews) starting Q2
  - Recruit members from across IT and business units
  - Achieve 30+ community members and 60% attendance at monthly sessions by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Program Manager + Senior Architect
- **Dependencies:** Marketing/socialization, compelling content

**KR 4.3: Build succession and capability depth**
- **Measure:** Develop at least 2 senior architects ready for EA leadership roles; cross-train team on core competencies
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Create individual development plans for all EA team members by Q2
  - Assign stretch projects/leadership opportunities to high-potential architects
  - Implement peer mentoring and architecture review rotation to build competency depth
  - Document core EA competencies and ensure every competency has 2+ team members proficient by Q4
  - Conduct leadership readiness assessment and identify succession candidates
- **Owner:** EA Director
- **Dependencies:** Stretch opportunities available, team members' willingness to develop

**KR 4.4: Expand EA capability through strategic hiring or upskilling**
- **Measure:** Add 1-2 FTE or equivalent capability (contractor, upskilled internal team member) with focus on demand management, business analysis, or portfolio management skills
- **Success Criteria:**
  - Assess team capability gaps by Q1
  - Develop business case for headcount or upskilling investment by Q2
  - Execute hiring or training plan by Q3
  - New capability is contributing to demand management or business partnership objectives by Q4
- **Owner:** EA Director
- **Dependencies:** Budget approval, HR support, competitive talent market

## OKR Operating Model

**Monthly Check-ins:**
- EA leadership reviews progress on all KRs
- Identify blockers and adjust tactics as needed
- Update dashboard for transparency

**Quarterly Business Reviews:**
- Present progress to IT leadership and key stakeholders
- Celebrate wins and learn from misses
- Adjust targets if business context changes significantly

**Individual Accountability:**
- Each EA team member owns specific KRs or contributes to team KRs
- Individual performance conversations tied to OKR contribution
- Recognition for KR achievement built into team culture

**Transparency:**
- OKR progress visible to EA team and stakeholders
- Use progress to tell EA's story and build credibility

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk: Business units don't engage with demand intake or partnership opportunities**
- Mitigation: Start with "friendly" business units; demonstrate value through pilots; secure executive sponsorship

**Risk: Team lacks time/budget for business acumen development**
- Mitigation: Blend low-cost learning (shadowing, lunch-n-learns) with targeted external training; prioritize this as team development investment

**Risk: Can't quantify EA value convincingly**
- Mitigation: Establish conservative methodology early; focus on most tangible value (avoided costs, schedule acceleration); collect stakeholder testimonials as qualitative evidence

**Risk: Tooling/platform limitations hinder demand management capability**
- Mitigation: Start simple (Excel/PowerBI) before investing in sophisticated tools; focus on insights over perfect data

**Risk: Team capacity stretched across too many priorities**
- Mitigation: Be disciplined about scope; it's better to excel at fewer objectives than underdeliver on many; use 70% target to allow for realism
